# Kong trademark & brand usage guidelines (verbatim)

Source: `https://konghq.com/company/branding?mode=agent` (Kong's own agent-readable brand/trademark page), cross-checked against the human-readable branding page and the 2026 v1.1 Brand Guidelines PDF. Kept verbatim here — quote or apply these directly rather than paraphrasing when the context is legal/commercial (partner agreements, press, external marketing).

## What counts as the Kong trademark

> The Kong trademark includes the Kong name & logo, and any word, tagline, phrase, image, or other designation that identifies any Kong products.

Don't modify the trademarks or use them in an altered way — including suggesting sponsorship or endorsement by Kong, or in a way that confuses Kong with another brand.

## Permitted uses

- Use "works with Kong" or "compatible with Kong" in text, if accurate.
- Link to Kong's site using the logo, if you're a Kong user.
- Educational, instructional use — eBooks, guides, publications, conference material.

**Educational materials must include this disclaimer verbatim** (fill in the title):
> "(Title) is not affiliated with or otherwise sponsored by Kong, Inc."

## Prohibited uses

- Editing, changing, distorting, recoloring, or reconfiguring the logo in any way.
- Using Kong's name in company names, service names, websites, trade names, or product names.
- Incorporating Kong's logo into another logo.
- Using domain names containing "kong" or a confusingly similar word.
- Kong branding on merchandise, t-shirts, or swag produced by a third party.
- Any confusing or misleading use suggesting Kong sponsorship or endorsement.
- Using Kong logos on a book cover without permission.

## Required actions

- **Check with Kong first** before using the logo on outside websites, products, packaging, manuals, or in any commercial/product context.
- Your own name/logo should be more prominent than Kong's in any co-branded placement.
- Include the affiliation disclaimer (above) in educational materials.

## Legal agreement (implicit, on use)

> "By using Kong brand elements, you agree to the Kong Inc. Terms of Service, these Kong branding guidelines, and all Kong rules and policies."

## Contact

For anything not covered here — an intended use that falls outside these rules, or a request to use the logo commercially — **design@konghq.com**.

## When to actually apply this file vs. just build on-brand material

Most of what you'll be asked to do (an internal deck, a customer-facing but internally-authored one-pager, a Kong employee's Slack graphic) doesn't trigger these trademark rules at all — they're about *external, third-party* use of Kong's name and marks, not Kong material made by Kong people. Reach for this file specifically when:
- Someone outside Kong wants to reference Kong in their own branding, docs, or marketing.
- You're producing something that will be publicly distributed and needs the disclaimer.
- You're asked to audit a partner/customer asset for trademark compliance (pair with `scripts/brand-audit.py` for the mechanical color/font checks, then walk this checklist for the usage rules a script can't verify — logo distortion, endorsement implication, name-in-domain, merch).
